Dogs as Pets: A Short History!

Have you ever wondered where dogs came from and how they became the most owned pet in the world? If so, welcome to Dogs as Pets: A Short History!

Although it may be hard to believe, your neighbors fluff ball Golden Retriever puppy was not always “Man’s Best Friend.” Dogs trace back to over 30,000 years ago when dogs had not yet split from their Gray Wolf ancestors. However, over thousands of years, dogs and wolves from Eurasia interbred with one another to form what we now know as the different dog breeds. However, what is most interesting is how over these thousands of years, dogs’ relationship with humans grew into one of companionship and love. Around 9,000 years ago, the domestication of dogs began when hunter-gatherer societies introduced them as hunting allies and protectors. Breeds, including German Shepherds, developed relationships with humans due to their herding abilities for livestock. Although some still use dogs for these purposes, dogs as we know it are known for their companionship and social role in human lives. In addition to being the most beloved pet in the world, dogs have been trained to serve as guides for blind and disabled owners, as therapy dogs in hospitals and nursing homes, and as police dogs! Humans learned throughout history that dogs could be bred specifically for these tasks; Although they have the same five senses as humans, dogs have an extremely efficient sense of smell and hearing, making them even better than humans at these tasks.

Benefits:

Taking a dog as a pet is very important decision for you and your future dog’s life. The choice should be taken with great consideration because taking good care of your pet is a very large commitment. However, taking a dog into your home has been scientifically proven to produce great benefits for you and your family! Bringing a dog into your life can be a great opportunity to create a real companion relationship to make you feel less alone. If you are struggling, dogs are known to bring people together and help prevent isolation! Even so, scientists have proven that having a dog can help you live longer and lower stress while bringing your family and friends together. Additionally, to other forms of service that dogs can provide, dogs also encourage a healthier lifestyle and can introduce daily movement into your life. Going on daily walks or runs with your dog can help your physical and mental health. Also, if walking or running alone is dangerous to you, having a dog can provide comfort and a sense of safety; Both at home and outside! With all these benefits in mind, there are still a lot of factors to consider before taking a dog into your home.
